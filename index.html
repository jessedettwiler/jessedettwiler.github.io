What is Folk-Hop?

Folk-Hop is a gentle meeting between two worlds: the folk culture of artists like Bob Dylan, and the lo-fi hip-hop legacy of J. Dilla.

It’s a warm, human form of instrumental music — built with acoustic guitars, ukuleles, flutes, banjos, harmonicas, kalimbas, mandolins, and quiet “la-la-la” voices that feel close and real. The drums come from hip-hop, but remain organic: handmade percussions, real kits, imperfect textures.

Folk-Hop is what Bob Dylan or Bon Iver might sound like if they lived inside chill beats. Music that breathes. Music that feels like a walk outside.

About

C4C is the artistic identity of Jesse Aymon Dettwiler. The name first appeared in 2017, during his earliest Folk-Hop experiments — it simply felt right, and never left.

Originally, C4C stood for “Creations For Christ” — a quiet reminder that music, for him, has always been tied to something greater than himself. Faith has remained a subtle but steady root in his creative journey.

Based in French-speaking Switzerland, between lakes and mountains, Jesse grew up in France with an Australian mother and a Swiss father. At eighteen, military service brought him to Switzerland — and it became home.

He draws inspiration from calm landscapes, silence, and slowness. He loves music that rests the mind rather than agitates it. Music that speaks without words.

Through his work, he hopes listeners feel safe — as if stepping outside mentally, breathing fresh air, letting stress fall away. A pause in a noisy world. An invitation to slow down, to rest, to reconnect.
